Background

Led Open Source Compliance (OSC) program for Lenovo Group across all product lines, including high-profile tablet platforms such as Tab P11 Plus.

Pain Points

  • Lenovo's tablet products had experienced GPL violation incidents
  • Severe communication gap between Engineering and Legal teams — Legal seen as "Department of No"
  • Late-stage compliance reviews resulting in costly code rewrites and delayed releases
  • High legal and reputational risk from uncontrolled use of open-source components

Goal

Build a standardized, shift-left OSC framework that protects the company from legal risk while maintaining development velocity.

Solutions

As Open Source Compliance Lead, I designed and rolled out a company-wide OSC process that acts as the bridge between R&D and Legal teams.

  • Created common language and 3-step review cycle (identify license → analyze usage → decide action) to eliminate misunderstanding
  • Introduced Red/Yellow/Green license classification system for instant decision-making (Permissive = Green/Auto-approve, Copyleft = Red/Forbidden)
  • Shifted compliance review from late-stage to Build phase with standardized submission requirements
  • Established dedicated cross-team communication channels and OSS Use Policy integrated into daily developer workflow

Outcome & Impact

Quality & Stability

Achieved consistent compliance across all product lines and eliminated previous GPL violation risks.

Risk Mitigation

Prevented potential legal actions and major reputational damage by ensuring 100% audit-ready status for all open-source usage.

Engineering Efficiency

Removed "speed bump" perception of Legal team; developers now know exactly what to submit and receive fast feedback.

Cross-Functional Leadership

Successfully aligned Engineering, Legal, and Product teams under one clear framework, enabling secure and faster innovation across Lenovo Group.

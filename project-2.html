Background

Multi-phase server platform testing (Leading / Trailing / MLK) across Genoa/EGS projects with extremely high test case volume and pass rates (SDV 91.5%, SIT 97.6%).

Pain Points

  • Severe over-testing with majority of cases showing zero failures
  • No systematic prioritization, leading to wasted engineering effort
  • Inefficient cross-project leveraging and lack of calculated risk acceptance

Goal

Build a data-driven, risk-based testing framework to eliminate redundant tests while preserving quality, targeting 43–48% reduction in total test workload.

Solutions

As Server Firmware Test Lead, I designed and implemented a comprehensive Leading/Trailing/MLK test strategy, shifting from volume-based to risk- and data-driven testing across firmware, hardware, and software domains.

  • Analyzed historical data to correlate test cases with actual bug discovery rates and identified 89.5% zero-failure cases for scope reduction
  • Created 4-level test case prioritization (Priority 1 Major → Priority 4 Optional) based on business impact and failure history
  • Introduced Hardware Leverage Rule and configuration classification to reduce total HW test configs by 10–15%
  • Developed Risk Assume Model combining Priority level with historical Failure Rate to classify tests as Must Test / Risk Assume-able / Risk Assume
  • Established cross-project leveraging mechanism with "leverageable tags" in test management system and dynamic adjustment between automation and manual testing

Outcome & Impact

Quality & Stability

Maintained full coverage on critical functions while eliminating low-value tests.

Engineering Efficiency

Projected 43–48% reduction in overall test effort; reduced HW configuration variants by 10–15% through standardized leveraging rules.

Risk Mitigation

Introduced calculated risk acceptance model, ensuring high-risk items remain fully tested while safely deprioritizing low-risk ones.

Cross-Functional Leadership

Rolled out new strategy across firmware, hardware, and software teams; model now used as standard for subsequent Genoa/EGS projects.
